## TOPIC: Indian Psychiatry Market — Competitor Platform Analysis

source: specs/001-patient-psychiatrist-match/research/psychiatry-sessions-india.md (Section 1)

Six major Indian telemental health platforms were analysed. Key findings:

Practo operates as a marketplace aggregator. Psychiatrists set their own fees. No formal
new-vs-follow-up distinction enforced by the platform. Session listed as "active for 60
minutes"; in practice new patients 45–60 min and follow-ups 20–30 min driven by doctor
preference. Pricing: initial INR 1,500–4,600 online; follow-up typically 20–50% lower.
Includes asynchronous doctor chat with 24-hour response window.

Lybrate is a similar marketplace aggregator with 150,000+ doctors listed. Single fee per
doctor; no session-type taxonomy. Offers free follow-up consultation within 7 days
(asynchronous chat only, not a full video session).

MFine (acquired by MediBuddy 2022) is hospital-network-integrated telemedicine. Patient
answers preliminary symptom questions, is matched to a psychiatrist, then has
chat/audio/video consultation. Average pricing approximately INR 800. Free follow-up
within 5–7 days.

DocVita is a curated marketplace with a Care Manager concierge model. Average 30-minute
session. Starting from INR 1,000. Distinctive feature: prescription sent to DocVita Care
Manager who forwards to patient's WhatsApp. Uses Google Meet or Zoom stated as unrecorded.

RocketHealth is the most clinically structured competitor. Digital-native, vertically
integrated mental and sexual health platform; not a marketplace. Symptom survey then
matched psychiatrist then pay then video then e-prescription with optional home delivery.
Has formal initial-vs-follow-up distinction enforced as product SKUs: Initial 45–60 min
at INR 1,800+; Follow-Up 20–30 min at lower price. Only Indian platform with formal
initial-vs-follow-up distinction enforced at product level. Direct model for our platform.

DocGenie is the only subscription/care-plan model found. Monthly subscription bundled
therapy and psychiatry. Basic INR 7,500/month (4 psychologist sessions + 1 psychiatrist
session + 8 feedback calls), Superior INR 9,500/month, Premium INR 12,500/month.
Coordinator handles all scheduling post-enrollment.

Amaha (formerly InnerHour) is the most clinical of all platforms. Founded by a psychiatrist.
Offers self-care tools, peer support, therapy, and psychiatric care online and in-person.
110+ psychiatrists and therapists; 35,000+ sessions per year; 15+ Indian languages.
Physical centres in Mumbai, Bangalore, Delhi NCR. Proprietary clinical protocols and peer
supervision.

Critical market gap: No major Indian platform formally structures the clinical flow into
named session types enforced at the product level — except RocketHealth. Our platform
differentiates by enforcing Initial Assessment / Follow-Up / Urgent Review as distinct
product session types with regulatory compliance (video-only for all v1 sessions; video
mandatory for Initial Assessment per regulatory requirement).

## TOPIC: Clinical Workflow — How Indian Psychiatrists Work

source: specs/001-patient-psychiatrist-match/research/psychiatry-sessions-india.md (Sections 2.1–2.4)

Initial consultation (first visit) takes 45–60 minutes. Per the IPS Consensus on Psychiatry
History and Diagnostic Formulation (Jan 2025), the structured agenda covers 16 elements:
sociodemographic details, presenting complaints, history of presenting illness, negative
history, past psychiatric history, past medical history, family history, personal history,
premorbid personality, substance use history, Mental Status Examination (MSE — 10 domains),
physical examination, investigations ordered, provisional/differential diagnosis (ICD-10 or
DSM-5), diagnostic formulation (10–20 sentence biopsychosocial), and treatment plan
(pharmacological + non-pharmacological + psychoeducation). For telemedicine first
consultations: identity confirmation is the first step; provisional diagnosis must be reached
before prescribing; physical examination is replaced by collateral history and video MSE.

Follow-up consultation takes 20–30 minutes. Standard agenda: status check since last visit,
medication adherence, side effects, symptom trajectory, life events since last visit, brief
focused MSE (change from baseline), medication adjustment decision, next follow-up scheduling,
between-visit instructions. Documentation uses SOAP notes format (Subjective, Objective,
Assessment, Plan).

Session type taxonomy in Indian clinical practice (no formal regulatory taxonomy exists):
(1) Initial/comprehensive assessment — 45–60 min — video mandatory (regulatory)
(2) Medication initiation review — 15–30 min — any mode — 1–2 weeks post new medication
(3) Follow-up/review — 20–30 min — any mode — ongoing care
(4) Medication review (stable phase) — 15–20 min — any mode — monthly/quarterly
(5) Crisis/urgent review — 30–60 min — video preferred — acute deterioration
(6) Caregiver consultation — 20–30 min — requires patient written authorisation

Between-session communication: 73.4% of Indian psychiatrists deliver prescriptions via
email/WhatsApp per IPS survey data. WhatsApp is the dominant between-session channel in
actual practice. Telemedicine Guidelines 2020 permit asynchronous text-based follow-up but
require video for initial consultations when new medications are being prescribed for the
first time.

## TOPIC: Drug Classification for Telemedicine Prescribing

source: specs/001-patient-psychiatrist-match/research/psychiatry-sessions-india.md (Section 3.1)

Per the Telepsychiatry Operational Guidelines 2020, drugs are classified into three lists:

List A — Prescribable in first and follow-up consultations via telemedicine. First
consultation requires live simultaneous video. Includes antidepressants (escitalopram,
sertraline, fluoxetine, paroxetine, venlafaxine, duloxetine, mirtazapine, imipramine),
antipsychotics (haloperidol, risperidone, olanzapine, quetiapine, aripiprazole), mood
stabilisers (lithium carbonate, carbamazepine, valproate, lamotrigine), limited
benzodiazepines (clonazepam, clobazam — exceptions only), non-benzodiazepine anxiolytics
(buspirone, hydroxyzine), cognitive enhancers (donepezil, memantine, rivastigmine), and
non-stimulant ADHD medication (atomoxetine).

List B — Follow-up only. At least one prior in-person or video consultation required.
Includes add-on medications to optimise treatment such as pregabalin, gabapentin, and
certain antipsychotic add-ons. Any mode permitted for the follow-up.

List C — Prohibited from telemedicine prescribing. Schedule X drugs under the Drugs and
Cosmetics Act 1940 and NDPS Act substances. Most commonly prescribed problem drugs:
diazepam, lorazepam, alprazolam (Alprax/Restyl), nitrazepam, chlordiazepoxide (highly
common in India), zolpidem, methylphenidate, modafinil, buprenorphine, methadone,
phenobarbitone, and depot antipsychotics. Alprazolam is one of the most commonly prescribed
anxiolytics in India; a patient with severe anxiety may need a benzodiazepine that the
psychiatrist legally cannot prescribe via telemedicine.

The platform hard-blocks all List C drugs via FR-044. List B is informational-only in v1.
Both lists are maintained in PlatformConfiguration to accommodate future regulatory changes.
Automated List B enforcement (contextual block based on session history) is deferred to v2.

## TOPIC: Valid Telemedicine Prescription Format

source: specs/001-patient-psychiatrist-match/research/psychiatry-sessions-india.md (Section 3.2)

Mandatory fields per Telemedicine Practice Guidelines 2020:

Doctor fields (all mandatory): full name, designation, qualification, MCI Registration
Number (must appear on prescriptions, websites, emails, and WhatsApp messages),
clinic/affiliation name and address.

Patient fields: name, age, address, phone and/or email, ID verification record.

Medication fields: generic drug names in CAPITAL LETTERS (mandatory; brand name optional
alongside), dose, frequency, duration, route of administration, special instructions.

Format: digital (e-prescription) or photographed/scanned signed physical prescription.
Transmitted via email, WhatsApp, or any messaging platform. Must carry doctor's digital or
photographed wet signature. Must include date of consultation.

Platform obligation: The platform must provide the technical mechanism for the psychiatrist
to generate and transmit prescriptions, and must retain a copy in the patient's clinical
record. This is implemented via FR-043 (e-prescription generation). Patient name, age, and
address are collected in FR-001k (mandatory profile setup step at first registration).

## TOPIC: Three-Phase Treatment Model and Session Frequency

source: specs/001-patient-psychiatrist-match/research/psychiatry-sessions-india.md (Section 4)

Per IPS Clinical Practice Guidelines, treatment follows three phases:

Phase 1 — Acute Phase. Goal: achieve remission. Duration: 6–12 weeks. Visit frequency:
week 1–2 post-medication start: review within 1–2 weeks; thereafter every 2–4 weeks until
stabilisation; high-risk patients within 1 week; post-hospital discharge within 1 week
(mandated).

Phase 2 — Continuation Phase. Goal: prevent relapse, consolidate gains. Duration: 16–24
weeks (total 6–9 months from treatment initiation for depression/anxiety; longer for
schizophrenia/bipolar). Visit frequency: every 4–6 weeks.

Phase 3 — Maintenance Phase. Goal: prevent recurrence, optimise long-term functioning.
Duration varies: first-episode depression with full remission 6–12 months then taper;
recurrent episodes (2+) 2–5 years; 3+ relapses or schizophrenia/bipolar often indefinite.
Visit frequency: monthly for therapy; every 1–3 months for medication reviews in stable
patients; every 3–6 months for very stable long-term patients.

Stability criteria: active psychiatric symptoms resolved or sub-clinical; no suicidal
ideation; medication tolerance established; social/occupational functioning recovering or
recovered; consistent medication adherence; no new psychiatric crises in prior 4–6 weeks.

NIMHANS TAC Clinic data: 67.77% of telepsychiatry patients had 1–3 consultations total
in a follow-up period; 13.33% had 4–6 consultations; 18.7% had 7+ consultations. Clinical
outcomes: 94.4% of schizophrenia patients and 81.71% of bipolar patients showed improvement
using a stepped-down frequency model.

Note: Treatment phase tracking (Acute/Continuation/Maintenance) with phase labels is not
exposed in the v1 UI — deferred to v2 (GAP-032). The v1 implementation uses a follow-up
interval picker (FR-046) instead: psychiatrist selects a recommended next session interval
(1w/2w/4w/6w/8w/3m/6m or specific date) in the session notes form, and the platform sends
a WhatsApp nudge to the patient on that date.

## TOPIC: MHCA 2017 Documentation Requirements

source: specs/001-patient-psychiatrist-match/research/psychiatry-sessions-india.md (Section 5)

For all outpatient psychiatric services, Form B records are mandated:
Form B-1: Outpatient record
Form B-2: Inpatient record
Form B-3: Therapy record
Form B-4: Psychological assessment record

Minimum content per encounter (outpatient): registration number and sociodemographic data,
presenting complaints, history, advance directive and nominated representative information,
investigation reports, treatment advice, psychological assessment (if done), therapy session
notes (if done).

Session-level documentation must include: type of treatment/therapy, duration and goals,
techniques used, clinical observations, progress notes, capacity assessment (when relevant),
risk/benefit discussions, consent status.

Patient data access right: patient can request records via Form A; must be provided within
15 days.

8. (LOW) Psychiatrist no-show detection relies entirely on Zoom webhook participant data

records.

extend this data model. Condition tag linkage in session notes would need to be added to
