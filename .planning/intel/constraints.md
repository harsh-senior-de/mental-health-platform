## CONSTRAINT-001 — All PHI Encrypted at Rest and in Transit

source: .specify/memory/constitution.md
type: nfr
All PHI and PII must be encrypted at rest using AES-256 (or equivalent) and in transit
using TLS 1.2 or higher. This applies to every data store and every network path that
carries user data. No exceptions.

## CONSTRAINT-011 — No Automated Clinical Diagnosis or Treatment Recommendations

source: .specify/memory/constitution.md
type: nfr
Automated systems must not provide clinical diagnoses or treatment plans. Only licensed
psychiatrists may make clinical decisions on the platform. This is an absolute prohibition
with no exceptions. The draft recommendation generated from the Zoom transcript (FR-015) is
an automated suggestion only — it must never be applied to the patient record without
explicit psychiatrist review and approval (FR-015a).

## CONSTRAINT-013 — RBAC Enforced at Service Layer; Psychiatrist Access Scoped Per Booking

source: .specify/memory/constitution.md + specs/001-patient-psychiatrist-match/spec.md
type: api-contract
RBAC must be enforced at the service layer — not only at the UI layer. Psychiatrists must
only access data for patients with whom they have a booking within the platform-configured
access window (default 3 months). Lateral access to other patients' data is strictly forbidden.
Raw session transcripts are per-pair only — a psychiatrist can only read transcripts from
sessions they personally conducted. AgencyAdmins are scoped to their own agency only.
Platform Admins have zero access to patient clinical data.

## CONSTRAINT-014 — Single Platform Zoom Business Account; All Transcripts via Webhook

source: specs/001-patient-psychiatrist-match/spec.md
type: protocol
All session meetings are created under the platform's single Zoom Business account via the
Zoom API. Patients and psychiatrists join as external participants via the generated meeting
link. No per-psychiatrist OAuth or Zoom sub-accounts. All transcript webhooks are received
on this single account. Zoom cloud recording and auto-transcription must be enabled on this
account. No per-psychiatrist Zoom credential management.

## CONSTRAINT-015 — Razorpay Payment; Three-Path Confirmation; Customer Money Never Held

source: specs/001-patient-psychiatrist-match/spec.md
type: protocol
Payment must be collected via Razorpay (INR). Booking confirmation must use a three-path
strategy (HMAC signature verification, webhook, reconciliation job). If payment is confirmed
but booking cannot be completed for any reason, an immediate automatic full refund is issued.
Customer money must never be held without a confirmed booking — no exception.

## CONSTRAINT-016 — Initial Consultation Must Be Video; v1 Is Video-Only for All Session Types

source: specs/001-patient-psychiatrist-match/spec.md (FR-042)
type: protocol
Per the Telemedicine Practice Guidelines 2020, the Initial Assessment session type is mandatory
for a patient's first consultation with any psychiatrist on the platform and must be conducted
via video. In v1, all three session types (Initial Assessment, Follow-Up, Crisis/Urgent)
use Zoom video exclusively. Audio-only and text-based consultation modes are deferred to v2.

## CONSTRAINT-017 — List C Drugs Prohibited from Telemedicine Prescribing

source: specs/001-patient-psychiatrist-match/spec.md (FR-044)
type: nfr
List C drugs (including but not limited to alprazolam, diazepam, lorazepam, nitrazepam,
chlordiazepoxide, zolpidem, methylphenidate, modafinil, phenobarbitone, and depot
antipsychotics) are legally prohibited from telemedicine prescriptions under the
Telepsychiatry Operational Guidelines 2020 and the Drugs and Cosmetics Act 1940.
The prescription tool must hard-block any attempt to add a List C drug. No override or
bypass mechanism exists. The List C list is maintained in PlatformConfiguration.

## CONSTRAINT-019 — Patient Data Portability and Deletion Within Defined SLAs

source: .specify/memory/constitution.md + specs/001-patient-psychiatrist-match/spec.md

## Open Regulatory Gaps (Not Constraints on v1 Implementation — Flagged for Planning)

The following gaps from the research document remain open and may introduce additional
constraints in the planning phase:

- GAP-032: No treatment phase tracking (Acute/Continuation/Maintenance) or follow-up

  specific session frequency requirements; the current spec does not model phases.
  source: specs/001-patient-psychiatrist-match/research/psychiatry-sessions-india.md (Section 4.1)

  must produce a Form B-1 outpatient record with mandatory content beyond what the current
  CareRecommendation entity captures.
  source: specs/001-patient-psychiatrist-match/research/psychiatry-sessions-india.md (Section 5)

- GAP-035: Caregiver Consultation type not modelled. The Telepsychiatry Operational
  Guidelines 2020 define a specific caregiver consultation type requiring patient written
  authorisation. Deferred to v2 per spec Future Readiness section.
  source: specs/001-patient-psychiatrist-match/research/psychiatry-sessions-india.md (Section 2.3)

- GAP-027: GST invoice sequential numbering. GST law requires sequential invoice numbers
  for B2C supplies; FR-041 currently omits this field.
  source: specs/001-patient-psychiatrist-match/spec.md (FR-041)
